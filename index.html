Bio

Hi, my name is Charlie Roscoe. I'm a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and Dana Farber Cancer Institute, Boston, MA, US. I'm originally from Manchester in the UK. I use geospatial tools to link physical, chemical, and social environmental exposures to large health datasets (e.g. individual-level cohort studies and multi-state cancer registries). In my PhD at Imperial College London, I modelled environmental exposures (e.g. air pollution, traffic noise, walkability, greenspace) at residential addresses of UK Biobank participants. In my Postdoc, I've done some big, time-varying exposure linkages (e.g. temperature), but I'm primarily focused on environmental epidemiology e.g. assessing cancer and cardiovascular disease risk. I co-chair the International Society for Environmental Epidemiology North American Chapter Policy Committee and my goal is to facilitate environmetal health equity through collaboration, communication, and advocacy. I'm looking for the right job.
