Bio

Hi, my name is Charlie Roscoe. I'm a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and Dana Farber Cancer Institute, Boston, MA, US. I'm originally from Manchester in the UK. I use geospatial tools to link physical, chemical, and social environmental exposures to large health datasets (e.g. individual-level cohort studies and multi-state cancer registries). In my PhD at Imperial College London, I modelled environmental exposures (e.g. air pollution, traffic noise, walkability, greenspace) at residential addresses of UK Biobank participants to assess associations with cardiovascular health. In my Postdoc, I've done nationwide (US), time-varying environmental exposure linkages (e.g. temperature) and am primarily focused on assessing cancer and cardiovascular disease risk. I co-chair the International Society for Environmental Epidemiology North American Chapter Policy Committee. My career goal is to facilitate environmetal health equity through collaboration, communication, and advocacy.

GPS-located exposure assessment

We passively collected Global Positioning System (GPS) data (10-minute intervals) using a custom-designed smartphone application and sleep data using Fitbit wrist-worn wearable devices (30-second intervals) from Mobile Health Substudy participants, embedded in the nationwide Nurses’ Health Study 3 cohort. We assessed greenness – based on GPS every 10 minutes during 7-day sampling periods across 4 seasons (2018-2020) – using Landsat satellite-derived 30 m resolution Normalized Difference Vegetation Index data (n = 447,120 GPS-located exposure points). Waketime and sleeptime greenness may impact sleep duration.
